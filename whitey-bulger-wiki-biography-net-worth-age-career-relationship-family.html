Whitey Bulger Wiki, Biography, Net Worth, Age, Career, Relationship,Family

Publish date: 2024-08-01

Whitey Bulger was a well-known figure in American organized crime, leading the Winter Hill Gang in the Winter Hill neighborhood of Somerville, Massachusetts. This article delves into his life, death, arrest, family, and net worth.

Whitey Bulger’s Passing

  • Bulger met his demise on October 30th, 2018, at the United States Penitentiary, Hazelton in West Virginia.
  • Inmates attacked him while he was in his wheelchair shortly after his arrival, leaving him unrecognizable due to the brutality.
  • His cause of death was later revealed as blunt force trauma.

The Arrest of Whitey Bulger’s Murderers

  • On August 18th, 2022, three inmates were found responsible for Bulger’s death: Fotios Geas (55), Paul J. DeCologero (48), and Sean McKinnon (36).
  • The trio faced charges of conspiring to commit first-degree murder.
  • Geas and DeCologero were accused of repeatedly striking Bulger in the head, ultimately leading to his tragic demise.

Whitey Bulger’s Background

  • Bulger, originally named James Joseph Bulger Jr., was born on September 3rd, 1929, in Boston, Massachusetts.
  • He headed the infamous Winter Hill Gang and was a prominent figure in organized crime.
  • He passed away at the age of 89.

Whitey Bulger’s Relationships

  • In the 1960s, Bulger was romantically involved with waitress and fashion model Lindsey Cyr, who later became his common-law wife.
  • His associate Catherine Greig, 22 years his junior, was another significant relationship. She faced legal repercussions for aiding Bulger and committing identity fraud.
  • Greig received an 8-year prison sentence in June 2012, along with a 21-month contempt charge. She was released in July 2020.

Whitey Bulger’s Family

  • Bulger had a son named Douglas Glen Cyr with his common-law wife, but tragically, the child passed away at the age of six due to Reye’s Syndrome.

Charges and Sentence

  • Bulger was arrested in 2011 after evading capture for 16 years.
  • He faced charges for nineteen murders, supported by testimony from Kevin Weeks and other criminal associates.
  • His charges included conspiracy to commit murder, extortion, narcotics distribution, conspiracy to commit money laundering, and racketeering.
  • Ultimately, he was found guilty of 11 murders, resulting in two life sentences and an additional five years. He was also ordered to forfeit $25.2 million and compensate victims with $19.5 million.

Whitey Bulger’s Influence on Cinema

  • A Hollywood film titled “Black Mass” (2015) portrayed Bulger’s life, with Johnny Depp playing the lead role. The movie enjoyed success at the Box Office.
  • Additionally, a documentary titled “Whitey: United States of America v. James J. Bulger” (2014) focused on Bulger’s trials.

Whitey Bulger’s Estimated Net Worth

  • Sources suggest that before his arrest in 2011, Bulger’s net worth was approximately $25 million, as estimated by prosecutors.

Conclusion

Whitey Bulger’s life was marked by his leadership in organized crime, his relationships, his criminal activities, and his eventual arrest and conviction. His story has been immortalized in both Hollywood and documentaries, leaving a lasting impact on popular culture.
