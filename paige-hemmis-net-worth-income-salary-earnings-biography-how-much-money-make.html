Paige Hemmis (born March 17, 1972 in Wisconsin, United States) is an American television personality best known as the host of the reality television series Extreme Makeover: Home Edition. She is also a model and actress. In 2001, she established a real estate business that specialised in the purchase and renovation of homes.

Paige saw a need in the market during her years working in the construction business, namely when it came to women’s equipment and goods. “I had a difficult time locating gloves and tool belts that were the right size for me. I would continually have to steal men’s clothing and drill additional holes or use duct tape to make it fit me in order to acquire the equipment I needed.” With this in mind, Paige created Tuff Chix, Inc., a company devoted to providing women with high-performance workwear that is both durable and stylish. Tuff Chix is a line of clothing inspired by Hemmis’ own work ethic and tenacity, and includes products such as pink work gloves, boots, and tool belts among others. 

It was in 2006 that she published her first book, The Tuff Chix Guide to Easy Home Improvement, with the goal of inspiring other women to take on their own home improvement projects with confidence. Her Tuff Entertainment Group is presently developing television and film projects, which she is currently involved in. During the 2009 season of The Superstars, she and her partner Bode Miller competed in a competitive reality programme, where they finished in second place. Besides that, she is a spokeswoman for a number of charitable organisations, including Habitat for Humanity and the Starkey Hearing Foundation. 

Paige regarded her appearance on Extreme Makeover: Home Edition to be a “blessing,” and she considered herself “very lucky” to be able to earn a career by assisting others. “It’s incredible that we have the chance to travel throughout our nation, assisting families in redefining their sense of belonging. It’s an honour to be of assistance, and the experience will have a lasting impact on my life.” His mother’s Catholic background, Hemmis explains in an interview with CBN.com, instilled in her a desire to be of service to other people. 

Hemmis was first intimidated by the commitment required of him to complete 40 hours of community service activity. She quickly recognised that volunteering was a worthwhile investment of her time and energy. “I remember thinking at the time how much of a hassle it was going to be. And, towards the end, thinking about how wonderful it made me feel to be able to give back to the community. As a result, I am grateful for my previous experience in providing assistance to others. The value of hard work has been ingrained in me from my childhood.”
