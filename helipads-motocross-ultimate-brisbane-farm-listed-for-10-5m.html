Helipads, motocross: Ultimate Brisbane farm listed for $10.5m+

Publish date: 2024-07-15

A farm within 20km of the Brisbane CBD that was once owned by a pub baron has been listed for sale for offers over $10.5 million, complete with helipads and a helicopter hangar, motocross track, sheds, tennis court and a luxe home.

Called Cloverleigh Farm, the vast holding sits on 152 acres in Bald Hills and has private access to the South Pine River.

It is being marketed by C1 Realty Beaudesert agent Daniel Bukowski as “the best kept secret in Brisbane”.

The main residence has three bedrooms including a master suite, open plan living areas that open out to an entertainment area with a commercial BBQ and fridges, a media room, two-car portico, a double garage, infinity edge pool and a full-sized tennis court.

A second residence also has three bedrooms and is fully self-contained.

Outside there is a helicopter hangar, two helipads, 10 horse/cattle yards, steel stock yards, two machinery sheds, a floating pontoon on the river, boat ramp, boat shed and a motocross track complete with its own sprinkler system.

The property sits across five freehold titles and is currently a “viable farming operation with endless possibilities”.

“The size and position also lends itself to things such as a subdivision, townhouses, unit complex, polo ground (subject to Council approval) or a private golf course,” the listing says.

The farm was previously owned by Comiskey Group co-founder Paul Comiskey, who is one of three family directors of the development company that is best known for its pubs (The Doonan, Sandstone Point Hotel, Eatons Hill Hotel and Samford Hotel) and resorts including the Big 4 Sandstone Point Holiday Resort.

The developer also has four new projects coming soon, including Aura Hotel on the Sunshine Coast, Strathpine Hotel, Dakabin Hotel and Coochin Fields, which will be a new festival site.

The Comsikey family’s former farm was sold for $7.2 million in 2016 to Eastern Energy Service Pty Ltd.

Mr Bukowski, who sold the former Comsikey farm almost seven years ago, said the current owners were selling as they had moved overseas.

He said the vendors had purchased another neighbouring block of land, boosting the landholding, which was also included in the sale.

It is the last remaining property owned by Eastern Energy Services Pty Ltd, which sold a luxury residence at Middle Ridge for $1.42 million in 2015, an industrial property at Torrington for $3.4 million in 2021, and another commercial property at Wilsonton for $3.4 million in June last year.
