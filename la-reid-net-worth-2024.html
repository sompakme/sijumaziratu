Early Life and Career Beginnings

L.A. Reid was born in Cincinnati, Ohio, and developed a passion for music at a young age. He began his career as a drummer for the R&B group Pure Essence before co-founding the famous R&B band, The Deele.

Establishing LaFace Records

In 1989, Reid and his partner Kenneth “Babyface” Edmonds founded LaFace Records. The label was responsible for launching the careers of several successful artists, including Usher, TLC, and OutKast.

Chairman of Island Def Jam Music Group

Reid’s success with LaFace Records led to his appointment as the chairman of Island Def Jam Music Group in 2004. During his tenure, he worked with and developed artists like Rihanna, Kanye West, and Justin Bieber.

Role at Epic Records

In 2011, Reid became the chairman and CEO of Epic Records, where he continued to influence the music industry by signing and developing artists such as Future, Fifth Harmony, and Meghan Trainor.

Founding Hitco Entertainment

After leaving Epic Records in 2017, Reid founded Hitco Entertainment, a label that focuses on developing new talent and producing hit records.

Reid’s Impact on the Music Industry

Throughout his career, Reid has been instrumental in shaping the careers of numerous artists. His keen eye for talent and ability to produce hit records have made him a respected figure in the music industry.

Reid’s Personal Life
