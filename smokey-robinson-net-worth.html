Smokey Robinson Net Worth: The Life and Legacy of the Music Icon

Early Life and Education

William Robinson Jr. was born on February 19, 1940, in Detroit, Michigan. He was raised by his mother, a schoolteacher and his father, who worked in construction. Robinson’s mother was a music lover and encouraged her children to cultivate their musical abilities. Robinson wrote his first song at age 16 and joined a local singing group. In high school, he formed The Miracles with his schoolmate Ronald White. After graduation, Robinson attended Northern High School and sang in the school choir, while also working at a drugstore.

Career

Robinson’s career took off when he met Berry Gordy Jr. in the late 1950s. Robinson and Gordy were both aspiring songwriters, and Gordy recognized Robinson’s talent. He signed The Miracles to his fledgling record company Motown, and Robinson became a songwriter and producer for the label, writing several hits for The Miracles and other Motown artists. Robinson and Gordy worked closely together, and Robinson played a significant role in shaping the Motown sound.

As a member of The Miracles, Robinson enjoyed considerable success in the 1960s, with hits like “Shop Around,” “You’ve Really Got a Hold on Me,” and “Going to a Go-Go.” He also wrote hits for other Motown artists, including The Temptations, Mary Wells, and Marvin Gaye. After leaving The Miracles in 1972, Robinson embarked on a solo career, releasing numerous successful albums, including “A Quiet Storm,” and “Being with You.”

Robinson was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1987 and has received numerous awards for his contributions to music, including a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2012.

Personal Life

Robinson has been married twice. In 1963, he married his fellow Miracle Claudette Rogers Robinson, and they had two children together. They divorced in 1986 after 23 years of marriage. In 2002, Robinson married his current wife, Frances Gladney, who he met while they were working on a charity event in Detroit.
