Vanessa Hudgens On Her Favorite Smoothie Recipe, Beauty Gadgets

Publish date: 2024-08-17

If you purchase an independently reviewed product or service through a link on our website, The Hollywood Reporter may receive an affiliate commission.

Newlywed Vanessa Hudgens is pledging to drink more in 2024 — of greens, that is. The Tick, Tick…Boom! actress is teaming with plant-based milk brand Silk and sharing her go-to breakfast smoothie recipe that includes peanut butter, cacao and a surprising ingredient inspired by her Filipino heritage: chickpeas.

“I always say breakfast is the most important meal of your day because it’s kind of going to jumpstart your entire day,” Hudgens tells The Hollywood Reporter. “We’ve all had those moments where we’ve eaten something very heavy and sugary and we’ve gone into our day dragging our feet, and I’m trying to rid myself of those days and start off on the right foot. And just incorporating some planty goodness into my breakfast is the perfect, easy way to kickstart my day. I think smoothies are a very easy, fun way to incorporate that into breakfast, and it’s really fun and easy.

The 35-year-old star (alongside Peloton instructor Robin Arzón and NFL pro-turned-commentator LaVar Arrington) is encouraging people to add more plants to their meals by joining Silk’s Feel Planty Good Challenge. Those who participate can enter to win a lifetime’s supply of free breakfast from Silk, and six more winners will get free breakfast for a year.

Of Silk’s non-dairy drinks, Hudgens notes, “this also has no added sugar, which is huge. And there’s 50 percent more calcium than milk, and there’s a ton of vitamins and minerals and protein, so it’s just a kind of no-brainer. And it’s so easy too. Yeah, you can make smoothies, but then you can also throw it into your coffee or into your cereal. There’s so many different options and ways that you can use it.”

Hudgens, who recently wed pro baseball player Cole Tucker in Tulum, Mexico, shared more about her “Beige Not Boring” smoothie, her dream Coachella lineup, her upcoming Hollywood projects and the skincare essential that’s a mainstay in her shower.

What’s your smoothie style? Are you an Erewhon type with everything in it but the kitchen sink? Or do you keep it simple?

I feel like I normally just do the exact same thing, but with the repetition, I feel like it can get a little boring, and that’s what I’m trying to not do this year! So I really ventured out and tried new things and gotten a little more adventurous with my smoothies. The smoothie that I actually created for this challenge is not your typical smoothie. It’s got some interesting ingredients in it, but it’s actually really delicious. So I’m excited for everyone to give it a try and let me know what they think.

It’s called Beige Not Boring — it has peanut butter and chocolate, but also chickpeas. I wanted to incorporate something that is from my [Filipino] heritage, so I pulled from the chickpeas and it’s really delicious and nutrient-rich. And with the Silk almond milk, it’s got 50 percent more calcium than milk and protein and vitamins. So I’m really jump-starting my day right.

Are the chickpeas a Filipino ingredient? I’m also Filipino, but my family doesn’t use them often.

Yeah! At least in my household there was. There’s a few great Filipino dishes that have chickpeas.

What’s your go-to blender right now for making smoothies?

I’ve got my Vitamix that I feel like has been the tried and true companion for smoothie-making, but I also have my Nutribullet on hand.

What does your daily routine look like these days?

Well, I definitely start my morning off with a plant-forward breakfast, and then getting some sort of active movement in, whether that’s a yoga class or a hike or indoor cycling or Pilates. It’s just a rotation between all of those. Those are the things that I love doing. I do them with friends, I feel like it’s always nice to have an accountability partner. Then do a little work at home on one of my companies. If there’s anything that needs to be focused on needs addressing, I’ll handle that then. And then catch up with friends. I feel like I spend a good amount of time with my friends and family and then dinner and then a bath because I’m a big bath person!

What projects do you have coming down the pipeline that you’re excited about?

We have still yet to finish Bad Boys we had a bit more filming to do on location before the strike happened, so that’ll be coming up pretty soon. But that’s always a fun franchise to be a part of. And I did a movie that I love called The French Girl with Zach Braff. It’s a great comedy and I get to play kind of a villain, but a really cute one! It’s fun to mix it up.

We’re big fans of your brand, Know Beauty’s face masks. What’s coming down the pipeline there?

We actually just launched our water purification line and I’m really excited and legitimately it use every single day. Being in LA, I didn’t realize how many toxins were actually in the water. And you can actually go [to the Environmental Working Group’s website] and type in your ZIP code and it’ll tell you what’s in the water and it’s really disturbing. We spend so much money on skincare products and hair products and all these things, but we’re not actually fixing the root of the problem, which is the water that we’re using. So I developed this water filtration system. It’s a shower head that is really easy to use and install, and it’s got some added goodness in there with hyaluronic acid and vitamin C just filtering out your water so that you’re actually getting clean water.

And it feels so luxurious, I love taking everyday life things and making it a luxurious moment for yourself, you step into the shower and it smells good, and the water pressure feels amazing with the shower head and you’re just getting good clean water and it’s helped my skin genuinely.

Festival season is coming up, and I know you’re a regular fixture. What are your spring music festival plans? Are you going to Coachella this year?

Honestly, I have no idea as of yet! I don’t think the lineup for Coachella has come out. For me, it’s genuinely about the music, so we’ll see. I haven’t seen any lineups yet, but once I do, I’m sure I’ll figure out the year. But I do love the festival and I do love live music. I feel like I missed out on a lot last year because I was filming.

And who are you hoping to see on the lineup?

For headliners, I would say the Radiohead. I’ve seen them headline Coachella, I think twice now. And I’m just obsessed. I’m the biggest Radiohead fan. I love them so, so, so, so, so much! Who else? I mean, I feel like I haven’t seen Megan Thee Stallion yet. That would just be so much fun because we all know the words. We all know the dances. Let’s do them! I would love that. And then maybe if anything was possible, Daft Punk. Get a little rock, a little hip hop, a little electronic.
